Subtracting the unseen sounds:
audio-visual target speaker extraction with reverse auditory selective hearing

-->

1. Abstract

Audio-visual target speaker extraction (TSE) aims at extracting the specific person's voice from the audio mixture given auxiliary visual cues. Previous methods usually search for the target voice through lip-speech synchronization. However, this strategy, which is referred as `addition' method, mainly focus on the target speech while ignoring the variations of the interference characteristics. This may extract the noise signal and result in speech extraction of an incorrect person in challenging acoustic situations. To this end, we propose an audio-visual TSE framework named TalkerExtractor with a `subtraction' strategy. Specifically, it is learned to utilize the interference signal through a reverse attention mechanism, which can suppress noisy sounds and avoid incorrect speaker extraction. The experimental results show that our system achieves state-of-the-art results on six datasets for both in-domain and cross-domain evaluation. Meanwhile, to simulate real-world conversation scenarios, we generate a new dataset, namely ASW-TSE, to support TSE on sparsely overlapped speech. We will release the codes, the models and data logs.
